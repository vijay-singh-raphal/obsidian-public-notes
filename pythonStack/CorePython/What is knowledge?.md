Knowledge is the familiarity, awareness, or understanding of facts, information, and skills acquired through, experience, education, or perception.
			Before understanding programming or AI concepts, we must know what kind of knowledge we are giving to the computer.
Broadly knowledge can be given in two ways.

1. <u>Imperative Knowledge</u> 
		- It tells how to do something.
		- It gives step by step instruction.
		- The computer follows the steps exactly.
		- Focus is on procedure or method.
		example -
	```python 
	sum = 0
	for i in range(1,6):
		sum = sum + i
			print(sum)
	```

2. <u>Declarative Knowledge</u>
		- It tells what the result should be, not how to get it.
		- No step by step procedure.
		- The system decides how to reach the goal.
		- Focus is no facts, rules, and relationships.
	```sql
	select name from students where marks > 60;
	```
